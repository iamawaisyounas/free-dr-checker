What Is Domain Rating?

Domain Rating (DR) is Ahrefs' score that measures the strength of a website's backlink profile on a scale from 0 to 100.

A domain rating checker is useful when you want a fast overview of how authoritative a website may be from a link building and SEO perspective. This free DR checker lets you enter a domain or full URL, cleans it into a valid domain, sends it to a secure backend endpoint, and returns the Ahrefs Domain Rating score in a simple results card. Because the tool uses the Ahrefs free Domain Rating API, it is built for quick checks without requiring a paid Ahrefs login inside the browser.

Marketers, founders, SEO teams, and content teams often use an Ahrefs DR checker before they evaluate guest post opportunities, compare competitors, review backlink quality, or prioritize outreach. A higher DR usually means that a website has a stronger backlink profile, but it should not be treated as the only measure of SEO value. You should also look at organic traffic, topical relevance, link quality, referring domains, content quality, spam signals, and whether the site attracts real visitors.

This website authority checker is designed for practical everyday use. If you paste a URL such as https://socialbu.com/, the tool removes the protocol, removes the trailing slash, converts the domain to lowercase, validates the domain format, and checks socialbu.com. The result shows the DR score, the checked domain, a status label, and an interpretation so the number is easier to understand. Scores from 0 to 10 are very low, 11 to 30 are low, 31 to 50 are average, 51 to 70 are strong, 71 to 90 are very strong, and 91 to 100 are elite.

When you check domain rating, remember that DR is not a Google ranking factor. Google does not use Ahrefs Domain Rating in its ranking systems. DR is an independent Ahrefs metric based on backlink profile strength. That makes it helpful for SEO research, but it is best used as a comparison metric rather than a direct prediction of rankings. A lower DR site can outrank a higher DR site when its content is better, search intent match is stronger, page-level links are better, or the topic is more relevant.

A free DR checker is especially helpful when you need quick checks at the start of a workflow. You can use it while building a prospect list, reviewing partnership requests, auditing your own domain, checking competitors, or estimating the authority of websites that mention your brand. It gives you a fast signal before you spend more time on deeper SEO research. For a complete analysis, combine the DR score with backlink audits, anchor text review, content gap research, keyword rankings, and manual quality checks.

If you are using this Ahrefs DR checker for outreach, avoid judging websites by Domain Rating alone. Relevance matters more than raw authority. A niche website with a moderate DR and a real audience can be more valuable than a very high DR website that is unrelated to your market. The best SEO decisions come from combining metrics with context, not chasing one number. Use this free tool as a clean first step, then inspect the sites that matter most in more detail.

FAQ

What is DR?

Domain Rating is Ahrefs' measure of backlink strength.

Is DR a Google ranking factor?

No. DR is an Ahrefs metric.

What is a good DR score?

Generally 50+ is strong.

How often is DR updated?

Depends on Ahrefs index updates.
